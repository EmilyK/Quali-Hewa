Meet The Team

Jeremy Anderson

Founder - Lead Product Designer & Engineer

jeremy@objectadjective.com Work 415-493-8489

Jeremy is the Founder and Lead Product Designer at Object Adjective. He has worked with a wide range of clients, including Twitter, Adobe, Zazzle, Chronicle Books and Sidereel, to name a few.

Over the past decade or so, Jeremy has helped both well established and startup companies take their ideas from sticky notes and whiteboards, to fully developed, robust products, that serve vast, specialized communities. His expertise ranges from user experience, user interface and interaction design, to web-based services, social applications and strategic development.

Jeremy is an active member in the web community and can often be found at local meetups and conferences in and around San Francisco. He studied new media art and design at The Baskin Visual Arts Center, at University of California, Santa Cruz.

Patrick Dowell

CTO, Lead Engineer

patrick@objectadjective.com Work

Patrick's background in mathematics gives him a sophisticated grasp on even the most theoretical aspects of programming, but his real passion comes from applying that knowledge to building computer applications and web services. He has developed his own set of sophisticated web development tools for organizations attempting to build their own applications.

Patrick graduated from Cornell University with a Bachelor degree in Engineering and a minor in mathematics. While at Cornell he taught academic workshop classes in computer science and helped found a game design initiative that eventually evolved to be a minor degree program.

In 2004 he was accepted into the prestigious Mathematics PHD program at Illinois, but it did not take him long to realize that academia wasn't for him; Patrick wanted to build things. After getting his feet wet working as a programmer, he joined Object Adjective to head up our backend development team.

Vara Pappas

Senior Designer, Photographer

vara@objectadjective.com Work

Vara has an extremely creative background with developed skills in design, photography, retouching, illustration, and beyond.

In 2000, she attended Savannah College of Art and Design on a portfolio scholarship. She discovered an intense love for photography, and received a great deal of recognition for her very first series. She also strengthened her illustrative skills, and began learning the fundamentals of design.

She later transferred to, and graduated from the Academy of Art University, in San Francisco. After receiving her BFA in Graphic Design, she furthered her education by returning in '07. This time she studied more about photographic lighting.

Prior to joining Object Adjective, Vara worked for Ad and Design firms creating and building brands, designing websites, advertisements, packaging, and doing collateral. She has also worked independently as a photographer and designer for a multitude of large and small businesses.

Dan Andreescu

Database Specialist, Engineer

dan@objectadjective.com Work

Dan graduated from Cornell University in 2004 with a Bachelor degree in computer science and a specialization in mathematics. While there, he published two articles on Code-project. He is an expert in the design, optimization and implementation of relational databases, as well as in solution architecture using the Microsoft and J2EE platforms.

Before joining Object Adjective, Dan worked for four years at the Tallan consulting firm. At Tallan, he was an integral part of major projects for a number of companies, including FedEx, Kinko’s, Sovereign Bank, and The Walt Disney Internet Group. During this time, he also designed and constructed the Galaxy Zoo website (and infrastructure), which received 6 million hits on its first day. He has 3 years of experience with standards-compliant user interaction design and programming, ample familiarity with AJAX coding (using standard libraries such as AJAX.NET, YUI, and prototype), as well as 3 years of experience with project management methodologies.

Dan has a passion for system building and loves to put his mind to work creating large-scale databases and other ambitious programming structures. His experience gives him a knack for constructing applications from the ground up and the foresight to carry those applications smoothly through all phases of the development process.

Sam Applebaum

Content Strategist

sam@objectadjective.com Work

Sam studied writing and film at Sarah Lawrence College. He works as both a screenwriter and internet marketer and has great passion for both disciplines. He brings both extensive internet marketing and copywriting services to Object Adjective and our clients.

His screenplays have received numerous awards (top 5% of about seven thousand scripts in the Nicholl Fellowship quarter finales as well as a second place finish in Writers Symposium) and glowing reviews from the top agencies (ICM, CAA and UA). His script “Three Stories About Joan” has received considerable attention.

Squiggy Monster

CSO, Community Manager

squiggy@objectadjective.com Work

Squiggy spent his formative years on the streets of Marin. Fending for himself, to see to food, water and shelter, afforded him a wealth of experience and street smarts. At a low point in his education, he was incarcerated by local pound authorities. His prospects looked dim, until the wonderful people at Big Dog Rescue, in Penngrove, took him in.

Shortly there after, Squiggy was eagerly adopted into the Object Adjective family, where his intuition and people skills became quickly apparent. He draws from his experience on streets and from the kindness of those who took him in, to head up both security and community management at Object Adjective.

In addition to his astute study of character, Squiggy has leveraged his afflictions for Guinness foam, chasing balls (and the occasional pigeon) and stealing any lap to be had, to secure a supremely safe, productive and friendly environment for both our clients and those of us here in the office.

We have been so impressed by his dedication and professionalism on and off the phone, that he has recently taken over management of several of our prestigious accounts.
